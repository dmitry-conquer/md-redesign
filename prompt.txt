You are a senior award-winning creative developer, digital art director, UX designer, interaction designer, and motion designer.

Your task is to design and build a premium, production-ready landing page with the visual ambition, polish, and interaction quality commonly found on Awwwards-winning websites.

The result must not feel like a template, wireframe, or ordinary corporate page. Every section should introduce a meaningful visual transition, spatial relationship, or interactive moment while maintaining clarity, usability, accessibility, and conversion focus.

## Creative direction

Create a sophisticated digital experience characterized by:

- Strong art direction and editorial composition.
- Large expressive typography.
- Deliberate use of negative space.
- Cinematic pacing.
- Layered depth and spatial transitions.
- High-quality image treatment.
- Restrained but memorable visual effects.
- Consistent motion language across the entire experience.
- Clear hierarchy and conversion-oriented UX.
- Premium interaction details at every stage of the journey.

Avoid generic cards, predictable section stacking, excessive gradients, random decorative shapes, and animation without purpose.

The experience should feel composed as one continuous visual story rather than a collection of unrelated sections.

## Motion system

Build a unified motion system rather than isolated animations.

Motion should feel:

- Smooth
- Weighted
- Cinematic
- Responsive
- Directional
- Physically believable
- Consistent across sections

Define a small collection of custom easing curves and reuse them throughout the experience.

Use Lenis to create controlled smooth scrolling and synchronize it with ScrollTrigger.

All scroll-driven motion must support reverse scrolling without visual jumps or broken states.

- Controlled tracking changes.
- Outline-to-solid typography transitions.

## Persistent 3D brand object

Integrate a supplied `.glb` model as a central part of the experience.

The 3D object must be rendered through a fixed transparent WebGL canvas and remain visible across the page.

### 3D rendering quality

Use a professional studio-lighting setup:

- PMREM-generated environment lighting.
- Neutral or brand-compatible tone mapping.
- Soft rectangular key, rim, and fill lights.
- Physically coherent materials.
- Controlled roughness and metalness.
- Restrained clearcoat.
- Consistent material treatment across front, back, and edge geometry.
- No accidental transparency.
- No cheap plastic appearance.
- No excessive bloom or overexposed highlights.

Preserve the supplied model instead of rebuilding it unless geometry repair is explicitly required.

Normalize the model, correct its visual proportions only when necessary, and establish a reliable local pivot.

### Scroll-linked 3D trajectory

Create responsive keyframe systems for desktop and mobile.

The object should:

- Begin from a meaningful interface position.
- Transition smoothly into the page.
- Move along intentional spatial paths.
- Rotate across multiple axes.
- Change scale.
- React subtly to scroll velocity.
- Maintain damped, inertial movement.
- Continue working during reverse scrolling.
- Avoid covering important content.
- Use different poses for different visual contexts.
- Remain performant on mobile.

Interpolate position, scale, rotation, and other pose properties with damped motion rather than snapping directly between states.

Use viewport-aware keyframes and recalculate them after resize and ScrollTrigger refresh.

Do not rely exclusively on hardcoded viewport percentages when the 3D object must align with a DOM element.

### DOM-to-WebGL alignment

When the model must align with a button, ring, logo, or other interface element:

1. Measure the real DOM target using `getBoundingClientRect()`.
2. Use the actual WebGL stage `clientWidth` and `clientHeight`, not only `window.innerWidth`.
3. Convert the measured DOM center into normalized WebGL coordinates.
4. Derive the 3D scale from the target’s real pixel dimensions.
5. Recalculate the result after resize, breakpoint changes, and ScrollTrigger refresh.
6. Account for scrollbar width and safe-area offsets.
7. When the model rotates, project its real geometry vertices through the active camera.
8. Calculate the visible 2D silhouette bounds.
9. Compensate the motion rig so the visible center remains aligned with the DOM target during rotation.

Do not manually tune fixed `x/y` values for critical DOM-to-WebGL alignment.

## 3D docking interaction

At an intentional point later in the journey, transition the 3D object into a compact fixed docking state.

The object should:

- Decelerate naturally into the dock.
- Scale down to fit completely inside a bordered interaction ring.
- Align its visible center with the ring’s center point.
- Preserve balanced clearance around the object.
- Continue rotating in response to further scrolling.
- Remain clickable through a transparent DOM interaction layer.
- Avoid blocking navigation or critical content.

Do not reveal the dock UI while the damped model is still approaching its final position.

Delay the ring and message reveal until the model has visually settled. Reverse scrolling should hide the dock immediately and return the object to its main trajectory.

## Conversion trigger and modal

Use the docked 3D object as a contextual conversion trigger.

Show a compact message near the object only after docking is complete.

On activation, open a premium lead-generation modal with:

- A cinematic GSAP entrance.
- A blurred backdrop.
- A refined panel transition.
- Clear visual hierarchy.
- A dedicated close control.
- Responsive internal spacing.
- A loading state for embedded forms.
- Mobile safe-area support.
- Correct focus management.
- Escape-key support.
- Click-outside closing.
- Focus restoration.
- Background inertness.
- Suspended smooth scrolling while open.

If a third-party form is embedded through an iframe, lazy-load it only on first interaction.

Because cross-origin iframe content cannot be styled from the parent document, create appropriate padding and visual framing around the iframe rather than attempting inaccessible internal styling.

## Navigation and stacking

Define a deliberate global stacking system.

The required order should be explicit:

- Base content
- Persistent 3D canvas
- Interactive 3D trigger
- Header
- Full-screen navigation
- Modal
- Loader and critical system overlays

The full-screen menu must always appear above the 3D object and trigger, including during its closing animation.

Do not remove the elevated menu state until the reverse transition has fully completed.

## Responsive behavior

Create dedicated desktop and mobile compositions rather than simply shrinking the desktop design.

On mobile:

- Simplify expensive or overly dense motion.
- Adjust 3D keyframes independently.
- Respect safe-area insets.
- Prevent fixed UI from blocking essential content.
- Convert horizontal pinned experiences into touch-scroll galleries where appropriate.
- Use responsive modal dimensions.
- Maintain readable typography.
- Prevent horizontal document overflow.
- Cap WebGL pixel ratio.
- Keep interactions touch-friendly.

Test at multiple desktop heights and widths, not only one large display.

At minimum, verify:

- Large desktop.
- Low-height desktop.
- Tablet-width viewport.
- Narrow mobile viewport.
- Tall mobile viewport.

## Accessibility

The experience must remain accessible despite its visual complexity.

Include:

- Semantic landmarks.
- Logical heading hierarchy.
- Descriptive alternative text.
- Keyboard-accessible navigation.
- Visible focus behavior.
- Correct ARIA states.
- Focus trapping for dialogs.
- Escape-key support.
- Focus restoration.
- Accessible names for icon-only controls.
- Reduced-motion behavior.
- Sufficient contrast.
- Non-hover alternatives for essential interactions.

When `prefers-reduced-motion` is active, remove or simplify nonessential animation without breaking content visibility or navigation.

## Performance

Maintain high visual quality without sacrificing responsiveness.

Use:

- Lazy-loaded media.
- Deferred third-party embeds.
- Controlled WebGL pixel ratio.
- Efficient Three.js geometry handling.
- Visibility-aware render loops.
- RequestAnimationFrame.
- Minimal layout reads inside animation frames.
- Cached measurements.
- ScrollTrigger refresh hooks.
- Passive event listeners.
- Efficient pointer interactions.
- GPU-friendly transforms.
- Responsive asset sizes.

Pause or reduce rendering when the document is hidden.

Avoid unnecessary textures, excessive post-processing, and continuous high-cost effects.
